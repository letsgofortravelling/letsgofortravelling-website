Top Destinations

Tungnath

Tungnath, located in Uttarakhand, is the highest Shiva temple in the world at 3,680 meters. Part of the Panch Kedar, it holds immense religious significance and is linked to the Pandavas' legend. The 3.5 km trek from Chopta offers stunning Himalayan views. Best visited between April and June, it connects to Chandrashila peak for breathtaking vistas. The region is rich in natural beauty, with diverse flora and fauna. In winter, the temple closes due to snow, but the trails remain popular among trekkers.

Adi Kailash

Adi Kailash, also known as Chhota Kailash, is a sacred mountain in Uttarakhand, India, revered as an alternative to Mount Kailash. Located at 5,945 meters in the Pithoragarh district, it is part of a spiritual journey that includes Parvati Sarovar, Gauri Kund, and nearby Om Parvat. Accessible via Dharchula, the trek is moderate and requires an Inner Line Permit due to its proximity to the Indo-Nepal border. Best visited from May to June or September to October, Adi Kailash offers breathtaking Himalayan views and a tranquil environment for spiritual seekers.

Mani Mahesh Temple

The Manimahesh Temple in Bharmour, Himachal Pradesh, is a revered shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, located near the sacred Manimahesh Lake and the Manimahesh Kailash Peak (4,080 meters). The temple is central to the Manimahesh Yatra, held annually during August-September, where devotees trek to the lake for ritual baths and blessings. The trek starts from Bharmour, passing through scenic landscapes, and is moderately challenging. Best visited during summer or the Yatra season, the temple is part of a tranquil and spiritual Himalayan pilgrimage. Nearby attractions include the Chaurasi Temple Complex and Kugti Wildlife Sanctuary.
